## How Governance Works

SuvrenHOA uses **transparent, blockchain-based voting** where every vote is recorded permanently and can be verified by anyone. This ensures complete transparency and prevents vote manipulation.

### Key Principles
- **One property, one vote** — Each property gets one vote regardless of owner
- **Transparent process** — All votes are public and verifiable
- **Immutable records** — Vote results cannot be changed once recorded
- **Democratic participation** — All property owners can propose and vote
- **Board oversight** — Board can intervene only in emergency situations

### Voting Schedule
- **Proposal submission** — Any time throughout the year
- **Voting periods** — Proposals open for 7-14 days depending on type
- **Results** — Automatically calculated and published
- **Implementation** — Approved proposals take effect immediately or as specified

## Proposal Categories

SuvrenHOA has **four categories** of proposals, each with different requirements:

### 1. Routine Operations
**Examples**: Maintenance schedules, vendor contracts, amenity rules
- **Quorum required**: 25% of properties
- **Approval threshold**: Simple majority (50% + 1)
- **Voting period**: 7 days
- **Board guardian power**: Can reject if violates bylaws

### 2. Financial Decisions
**Examples**: Budget changes, special assessments, dues adjustments
- **Quorum required**: 35% of properties
- **Approval threshold**: 60% supermajority
- **Voting period**: 10 days
- **Board guardian power**: Can reject if exceeds financial limits

### 3. Governance Changes
**Examples**: Bylaw amendments, policy changes, committee structure
- **Quorum required**: 50% of properties
- **Approval threshold**: 65% supermajority
- **Voting period**: 14 days
- **Board guardian power**: Can reject if violates state law

### 4. Constitutional Changes
**Examples**: Fundamental structure changes, voting system modifications
- **Quorum required**: 67% of properties
- **Approval threshold**: 75% supermajority
- **Voting period**: 14 days
- **Board guardian power**: Cannot reject (community decides)

## Quorum Requirements

**Quorum** is the minimum number of properties that must vote for a proposal to be valid.

### How Quorum Works
- Calculated as **percentage of total properties** in the community
- **Abstentions count** toward quorum (participating but not choosing)
- **Non-votes don't count** toward quorum
- If quorum isn't met, proposal **automatically fails**
- **Vote delegation counts** toward quorum if delegated voter participates

### Current Community Stats
- **Total properties**: 247
- **Active voters** (last 90 days): 189 (76%)
- **Average participation**: 68%

### Meeting Quorum
If a proposal is struggling to meet quorum:
- **Automatic reminders** sent 48 and 24 hours before deadline
- **Community notifications** posted in forum and announcements
- **Board outreach** — Board members may encourage participation
- **Deadline extension** — Board can extend voting by 48 hours if close to quorum

## Creating a Proposal

Any property owner can create a proposal following these steps:

### Step 1: Prepare Your Proposal
1. **Research the topic** — Check existing policies and past proposals
2. **Define clear objectives** — What exactly are you proposing?
3. **Consider implications** — How will this affect the community?
4. **Review category requirements** — Ensure you understand voting thresholds
5. **Gather support** — Discuss with neighbors to build consensus

### Step 2: Submit Through Platform
1. Go to **"Governance"** → **"Create Proposal"**
2. **Select category** — Choose appropriate proposal type
3. **Write title** — Clear, descriptive summary
4. **Provide detailed description**:
   - Problem or opportunity
   - Proposed solution
   - Expected benefits
   - Cost implications
   - Implementation timeline
5. **Add supporting documents** — Upload relevant files
6. **Set voting options** — Usually Yes/No/Abstain
7. **Review and submit**

### Step 3: Board Review Process
1. **Initial review** — Board checks for completeness and category accuracy
2. **Legal compliance** — Verify proposal doesn't violate laws or bylaws
3. **Financial impact** — Assess budget implications
4. **Community notification** — Announce proposal to all residents
5. **Voting period begins** — Usually within 48 hours of approval

### Proposal Requirements
All proposals must include:
- **Clear problem statement**
- **Specific proposed solution**
- **Financial impact assessment**
- **Implementation timeline**
- **Success metrics** (how will we know it worked?)

### Common Rejection Reasons
- **Violates state law** or local regulations
- **Exceeds financial authority** without proper approval
- **Duplicate of recent proposal** (within 6 months)
- **Incomplete information** or unclear objectives
- **Personal attacks** or inappropriate content

## How to Vote

### Casting Your Vote
1. Go to **"Governance"** → **"Active Proposals"**
2. Click on the proposal you want to vote on
3. **Read the full proposal** and any discussion comments
4. **Select your choice**:
   - **Yes** — You support the proposal
   - **No** — You oppose the proposal
   - **Abstain** — You participate but don't choose (counts toward quorum)
5. **Add optional comment** explaining your reasoning
6. **Confirm vote** in your connected wallet
7. **Pay small transaction fee** (usually under $1)

### Vote Privacy
- **Votes are public** — Anyone can see how you voted
- **Comments are optional** — You don't have to explain your reasoning
- **Voting history** — All your votes are permanently recorded
- **No vote buying** — Selling votes violates community bylaws

### Changing Your Vote
- **Votes are final** once submitted to the blockchain
- **No vote changes** allowed after submission
- **Think carefully** before voting
- **Ask questions** in proposal comments if unsure

### Vote Verification
You can verify your vote was recorded correctly:
1. Go to **"Governance"** → **"My Voting History"**
2. Click on the proposal
3. View **blockchain transaction** link
4. Confirm your vote appears in the public record

## Vote Delegation

If you can't actively participate in voting, you can delegate your vote to a trusted community member.

### How Delegation Works
- **Delegate your voting power** to another property owner
- **Delegate votes on all proposals** until you revoke delegation
- **You retain ownership** — Delegation doesn't transfer property rights
- **Revoke anytime** — Take back your voting power whenever you want

### Setting Up Delegation
1. Go to **"Governance"** → **"Vote Delegation"**
2. **Choose your delegate** from the list of eligible voters
3. **Confirm delegation** in your wallet
4. **Notify your delegate** — Let them know they can vote for you
5. **Automatic activation** — Takes effect for the next proposal

### Delegate Responsibilities
If someone delegates their vote to you:
- **Vote in good faith** — Consider their interests, not just your own
- **Stay informed** — Read proposals carefully before voting
- **Communicate** — Discuss major decisions with those who delegated to you
- **Act responsibly** — Delegation is a trust, don't abuse it

### Revoking Delegation
1. Go to **"Governance"** → **"Vote Delegation"**
2. Click **"Revoke Delegation"**
3. **Confirm in wallet** — Submit revocation transaction
4. **Immediate effect** — You can vote on the next proposal

### Delegation Limits
- **No chain delegation** — Can't delegate to someone who has delegated to someone else
- **Maximum 10 delegations** — Each person can receive votes from up to 10 properties
- **Board members can't receive** delegations to prevent conflicts of interest

## Voting Power Tiers

All property owners have equal voting power — **one property, one vote**. However, there are some nuances:

### Standard Voting Power
- **Property owners** — 1 vote per property owned
- **Multiple properties** — 1 vote per property (if you own multiple lots)
- **Rental properties** — Landlord votes, not tenant
- **Co-ownership** — Must designate one person to vote

### Special Situations
**New property owners**:
- **Full voting rights** immediately upon transfer
- **No waiting period** for participation
- **Must complete account setup** to vote

**Properties with violations**:
- **Maintain voting rights** even with outstanding violations
- **Cannot vote on** proposals if dues are >90 days overdue
- **Rights restored** immediately upon payment

**Vacant properties**:
- **Owner retains voting rights** regardless of occupancy
- **Property management companies** can vote if authorized
- **Bank-owned properties** (foreclosures) cannot vote until resold

### Corporate Ownership
If a property is owned by a corporation or LLC:
- **Designated representative** must be appointed
- **Authorization documents** required to verify voting authority
- **One vote per property** regardless of corporate structure
- **Representative can be changed** with proper documentation

## Proposal Lifecycle

Understanding how proposals move through the system:

### 1. Submission Phase
- **Proposal submitted** by community member
- **Initial review** by board within 48 hours
- **Category assignment** and quorum requirements set
- **Community notification** sent to all residents

### 2. Discussion Phase
- **Comment period** opens for community discussion
- **Questions answered** by proposal author
- **Information gathering** — Additional details may be requested
- **Amendments possible** if requested by board or significant community feedback

### 3. Voting Phase
- **Voting opens** once discussion period ends
- **Daily participation updates** sent to community
- **Quorum tracking** publicly visible
- **Automatic reminders** sent as deadline approaches

### 4. Results Phase
- **Voting closes** at specified deadline
- **Automatic calculation** of results
- **Immediate publication** of outcome
- **Blockchain verification** available for all votes

### 5. Implementation Phase
**If approved**:
- **Implementation timeline** begins as specified in proposal
- **Progress tracking** in community dashboard
- **Regular updates** on implementation status
- **Success measurement** against stated objectives

**If rejected**:
- **Results explanation** provided to community
- **Feedback analysis** — Why did it fail?
- **Resubmission guidelines** — When and how to try again
- **Alternative solutions** discussion in community forum

## Guardian Powers

The board has limited **guardian powers** to protect the community from proposals that could cause harm:

### When Board Can Intervene
**Mandatory rejections**:
- **Violates state or federal law**
- **Exceeds financial authority** without proper approvals
- **Conflicts with existing contracts** that can't be modified
- **Creates legal liability** for the HOA

**Discretionary rejections** (board vote required):
- **Violates HOA bylaws** or CC&Rs
- **Creates safety hazards** for residents
- **Exceeds budget authority** by more than 20%
- **Conflicts with** recently approved proposals

### Board Cannot Reject
- **Constitutional changes** — Community has final say
- **Proposals they personally disagree with** — Must respect democratic will
- **Proposals that criticize** board performance
- **Changes to board powers** — Only community can modify governance structure

### Guardian Appeal Process
If the board rejects your proposal using guardian powers:

1. **Request detailed explanation** — Board must provide written reasoning
2. **Appeal to full board** — Request formal board vote with minutes
3. **Community discussion** — Raise concerns in public forum
4. **Modify and resubmit** — Address board's specific concerns
5. **Legal review** — Contest if you believe rejection violates bylaws

The guardian power system balances community democracy with necessary oversight to protect everyone's interests.
