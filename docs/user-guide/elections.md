## Board Structure

The SuvrenHOA board consists of five elected positions, each with specific responsibilities:

### Elected Positions

**President**
- **Term**: 2 years
- **Responsibilities**: Executive leadership, community representation, vendor relations
- **Powers**: Proposal guardian authority, emergency spending up to $5,000
- **Meeting role**: Chairs board meetings and annual community meetings

**Vice President**  
- **Term**: 2 years
- **Responsibilities**: Assists president, leads special committees
- **Powers**: Acts as president when needed, secondary approval authority
- **Meeting role**: Facilitates community input sessions

**Treasurer**
- **Term**: 2 years  
- **Responsibilities**: Financial oversight, budget management, dues collection
- **Powers**: Treasury transaction approval, financial reporting
- **Meeting role**: Presents financial reports and budget proposals

**Secretary**
- **Term**: 2 years
- **Responsibilities**: Record keeping, meeting minutes, community communications
- **Powers**: Document management, correspondence authority
- **Meeting role**: Records minutes and manages meeting logistics

**Member-at-Large**
- **Term**: 1 year
- **Responsibilities**: General board duties, special projects, community liaison
- **Powers**: Equal voting on all board decisions
- **Meeting role**: Represents broader community perspective

### Board Powers and Limits
**What the board can do**:
- Approve routine operational expenses up to $10,000
- Enter into contracts up to $25,000 with community approval
- Hire and manage service contractors
- Enforce community rules and assess fines
- Emergency spending up to $15,000 (requires full board vote)

**What requires community vote**:
- Budget changes over $25,000
- Special assessments
- Bylaw or policy changes
- Major capital improvements
- Dues increases above annual inflation adjustment

## Election Schedule

Elections follow a predictable annual schedule to ensure continuity:

### Annual Election Timeline

**January**: 
- Election planning begins
- Nomination committee appointed
- Voter roll verification starts

**February**:
- Nomination period opens (February 1-28)
- Candidate information sessions held
- Campaign guidelines distributed

**March**:
- Nomination deadline (March 1)
- Candidate verification and approval
- Campaign period begins (March 15-31)

**April**:
- Voting period (April 1-15)
- Candidate forums and debates
- Community Q&A sessions

**May**:
- Results announced (May 1)
- New board orientation
- Transition meetings with outgoing members

**June**:
- New board takes office (June 1)
- Committee assignments finalized
- Annual meeting preparation

### Staggered Terms
To ensure continuity, board positions have staggered terms:

**Even years** (2026, 2028, etc.):
- President election
- Treasurer election
- Member-at-Large election

**Odd years** (2027, 2029, etc.):
- Vice President election
- Secretary election
- Member-at-Large election

The Member-at-Large position is elected annually to provide fresh perspective.

## Nomination Process

Any property owner in good standing can run for the board.

### Eligibility Requirements
**Must have**:
- Property ownership for at least 6 months
- Current on all HOA dues and assessments
- No major HOA violations in past 12 months
- Completed board candidate orientation session

**Cannot run if**:
- Currently in legal dispute with HOA
- Delinquent on property taxes
- Convicted of felony involving financial crimes
- Currently employed by HOA vendors or contractors

### Nomination Steps

**Step 1: Declaration of Intent**
1. Go to **"Community"** → **"Elections"** → **"Declare Candidacy"**
2. **Select position** you're running for
3. **Confirm eligibility** — System checks your account status
4. **Pay filing fee** — $25 in USDC (refunded if you receive >10% of votes)
5. **Submit initial declaration**

**Step 2: Required Documentation**
Upload the following within 7 days:
- **Background disclosure** — Any conflicts of interest
- **Platform statement** — Your goals and qualifications (max 500 words)
- **Professional photo** — For ballot and campaign materials
- **Contact information** — How voters can reach you

**Step 3: Community Support**
Gather **endorsements from 10 different properties**:
1. **Share your endorsement link** with neighbors
2. **Property owners sign endorsements** through the platform
3. **10 unique endorsements required** within 14 days
4. **Automatic verification** — System confirms endorser eligibility

**Step 4: Board Verification**
- **Eligibility confirmation** within 5 business days
- **Document review** for completeness and accuracy
- **Background check** for any disqualifying issues
- **Approval or rejection** with detailed reasoning

### Self-Nomination vs Committee Nomination
**Self-nomination** (most common):
- Follow standard process above
- Build your own support network
- Manage your own campaign

**Nomination committee recruitment**:
- Committee may approach qualified candidates
- Streamlined endorsement process
- Committee assistance with campaign basics
- Still requires full nomination documentation

## Candidate Endorsements

Endorsements help build credibility and demonstrate community support.

### Types of Endorsements

**Individual Property Owner**:
- **Single endorsement** per property for each candidate
- **Must be in good standing** with HOA
- **Public record** — All endorsements are visible
- **Can endorse multiple candidates** for different positions

**Organization Endorsements**:
- **Community groups** (book club, garden club, etc.)
- **Business associations** within the community
- **Volunteer committees** and boards
- **Must represent multiple community members**

**Current Board Member**:
- **Can endorse candidates** for positions they're not running for
- **Must clearly state** endorsement is personal, not official board position
- **Cannot use board resources** for campaign activities

### Endorsement Process
1. **Candidates request endorsements** from individuals or groups
2. **Endorsers submit through platform** or at community events
3. **Automatic verification** of endorser eligibility
4. **Public listing** of all endorsements on candidate pages
5. **Endorsements remain active** until election day

### Endorsement Guidelines
**Acceptable endorsement activities**:
- Recommending candidates in community forum
- Sharing candidate information with neighbors
- Attending candidate events and voicing support
- Displaying candidate information in your own yard

**Prohibited activities**:
- Using HOA resources for campaigning
- Offering financial incentives for endorsements
- Harassing residents for endorsements
- Making false statements about opponents

## Voting for Officials

Board elections use **ranked-choice voting** to ensure winners have broad community support.

### How Ranked-Choice Voting Works
1. **Rank candidates** in order of preference (1st choice, 2nd choice, etc.)
2. **First round** counts all first-choice votes
3. **If no majority** (50% + 1), lowest candidate eliminated
4. **Votes transfer** to next choice on eliminated candidate's ballots
5. **Process repeats** until someone has majority

### Voting Process
1. Go to **"Community"** → **"Elections"** → **"Cast Ballot"**
2. **Review all candidates** — Read platforms and endorsements
3. **Rank your choices**:
   - Drag candidates in order of preference
   - Must rank at least 3 candidates (if available)
   - Can rank all candidates if desired
4. **Review your ballot** carefully
5. **Submit vote** through connected wallet
6. **Receive confirmation** — Save transaction hash for verification

### Ballot Information
Each candidate listing shows:
- **Name and photo**
- **Platform summary** (abbreviated)
- **Key endorsements** 
- **Experience highlights**
- **Link to full platform** and background

### Voting Security
- **Blockchain verification** — Every vote permanently recorded
- **Anonymous voting** — Cannot trace individual votes to specific properties
- **Vote verification** — Can confirm your vote was counted correctly
- **No vote changes** — Final once submitted

### Election Results
Results are calculated automatically and announced immediately:
- **Round-by-round breakdown** showing vote transfers
- **Final vote counts** for all candidates
- **Margin of victory** for winning candidates
- **Participation statistics** for the community

## Term Limits & Succession

SuvrenHOA has term limits to encourage fresh leadership and prevent power concentration.

### Term Limits
**Standard limits**:
- **President/VP/Treasurer/Secretary**: Maximum 2 consecutive terms (4 years)
- **Member-at-Large**: Maximum 3 consecutive terms (3 years)
- **Total board service**: Maximum 8 years in any 12-year period

**Waiting periods**:
- Must wait **1 full term** before running for same position again
- Can run for **different positions** immediately
- **No lifetime limits** — Can return after waiting period

### Succession Planning
**If a board member resigns**:
1. **Immediate notification** to community
2. **Interim appointment** by remaining board (if <6 months left in term)
3. **Special election** required if >6 months remaining
4. **Appointment criteria**: Previous election runner-up given first option

**President succession**:
- **Vice President** automatically becomes interim president
- **Special election** within 60 days if >1 year remaining
- **VP completes term** if <1 year remaining

**Multiple vacancies**:
- **Emergency board** appointed by remaining members
- **Community-wide special election** within 45 days
- **Professional management** may be hired temporarily

### Transition Process
**Outgoing board members**:
- **30-day transition period** with incoming members
- **Document handover** — Passwords, contacts, ongoing projects
- **Knowledge transfer** meetings
- **Final reporting** on key initiatives

**Incoming board members**:
- **Orientation session** covering legal responsibilities
- **Platform training** on governance tools
- **Introduction meetings** with key vendors and contractors
- **Goal-setting session** for upcoming term

## Special Elections

Special elections may be called for various reasons beyond regular succession.

### When Special Elections Occur
**Mandatory special elections**:
- **Recall petition** approved (requires 30% of properties)
- **Board member resigned** with >6 months remaining
- **Board member removed** for cause
- **New position created** by community vote

**Optional special elections**:
- **Board expansion** approved by community
- **Position responsibilities changed** significantly
- **Community requests** major leadership change

### Recall Elections
Community can recall board members who fail to perform duties:

**Recall process**:
1. **Petition filing** — Must state specific cause for recall
2. **Signature collection** — 30% of properties must sign petition
3. **Board response period** — 14 days for member to respond to charges
4. **Special election** — Community votes on recall + replacement

**Valid recall reasons**:
- Failure to attend >50% of board meetings
- Violation of fiduciary duty
- Misuse of HOA funds or resources
- Conflict of interest violations
- Failure to enforce community rules consistently

### Emergency Leadership
In crisis situations requiring immediate board action:

**Emergency protocols**:
- **Interim board** appointed by largest vote-getters from last election
- **Limited authority** — Can only address specific emergency
- **60-day maximum** — Must hold special election within 60 days
- **Community oversight** — All decisions subject to immediate community review

## Campaign Guidelines

Fair and transparent campaigns benefit the entire community.

### Campaign Rules
**Allowed activities**:
- Door-to-door canvassing (during reasonable hours)
- Yard signs on your own property
- Social media promotion
- Candidate forums and debates
- Distribution of campaign literature
- Email newsletters to supporters

**Prohibited activities**:
- Using HOA email lists or contact information
- Campaigning in common areas without permission
- Negative campaigning or personal attacks
- Vote buying or quid pro quo arrangements
- False statements about opponents
- Campaigning within 100 feet of voting locations

### Campaign Finance
**Spending limits**:
- **Individual candidates**: $500 maximum per campaign
- **Must use own funds** or small individual donations (<$50 each)
- **No corporate donations** or contractor contributions
- **Full disclosure required** of all contributions and expenses

**Financial reporting**:
- **Pre-election report** due 7 days before voting
- **Final report** due 30 days after election
- **Public disclosure** of all contributions and expenditures
- **Penalties** for non-compliance include vote invalidation

### Campaign Support
**Community resources**:
- **Candidate forums** hosted by HOA (neutral moderation)
- **Voting guide** with all candidate information
- **Online platform** for candidate profiles and platforms
- **Community calendar** for campaign events

**Violation reporting**:
- **Report violations** through platform or to election committee
- **Investigation process** — Committee reviews within 48 hours
- **Penalties**: Warning, public correction, vote invalidation
- **Appeal process** available for all decisions
