## Your Property NFT

Your home ownership in SuvrenHOA is represented by a **Non-Fungible Token (NFT)** — a unique digital certificate stored on the blockchain.

### What Your Property NFT Contains
- **Legal property description** — Lot number, boundaries, square footage
- **Ownership history** — Complete chain of ownership
- **Property characteristics** — Home details, year built, features
- **HOA compliance status** — Current standing with community rules
- **Outstanding obligations** — Any dues, fines, or assessments

### Viewing Your NFT
1. Go to **"My Property"** in the main menu
2. Your NFT details appear at the top of the page
3. Click **"View Full NFT Details"** for complete information
4. The NFT ID is your unique property identifier (keep this number handy)

### NFT Benefits
- **Permanent record** — Your ownership can never be lost or altered
- **Instant verification** — Anyone can verify your property ownership
- **Transfer security** — Built-in safety measures prevent accidental transfers
- **Historical tracking** — Complete property history is always available

Some information can be updated by homeowners:

1. Click **"Edit Property Details"**
2. Update changeable fields like:

**Note**: Structural information (square footage, lot size) cannot be changed without official surveys and board approval.

## Property Transfer Process

When selling or transferring your property, SuvrenHOA has built-in safety measures.

### Preparing for Transfer
1. **Clear all outstanding dues** — No transfers with overdue payments
2. **Resolve any violations** — Property must be in good standing
3. **Complete pending maintenance** — All work orders must be closed
4. **Notify the board** — Give 30 days advance notice when possible

### Initiating Transfer
1. Go to **"My Property"** → **"Transfer Property"**
2. Enter the **new owner's wallet address**
3. Confirm the **transfer amount** (if applicable)
4. Review all **transfer conditions**
5. Click **"Initiate Transfer"**

### Safety Cooling Period
SuvrenHOA includes a **48-hour cooling period** for all transfers:
- Transfer is announced but not completed immediately
- You can cancel within 48 hours if needed
- Board members can intervene if they spot issues
- After 48 hours, transfer automatically completes

### What the New Owner Needs
Before accepting transfer, the new owner must:
- Create a SuvrenHOA account
- Connect their digital wallet
- Verify their identity with the board
- Acknowledge HOA bylaws and rules
- Pay any required transfer fees

### Emergency Transfer Recovery
If you accidentally initiate a transfer:
1. **Act quickly** — You have 48 hours to cancel
2. Go to **"My Property"** → **"Pending Transfers"**
3. Click **"Cancel Transfer"** next to the pending item
4. Confirm cancellation in your wallet

If you lose wallet access, see the [Security & Wallet Safety](security-wallet.md) guide for recovery options.

## Property Inspections

The HOA conducts regular property inspections to maintain community standards.

### Scheduled Inspections
- **Annual compliance review** — Every property inspected once per year
- **Pre-sale inspection** — Required before any property transfer
- **Post-violation follow-up** — After correcting reported issues
- **Random safety checks** — Periodic inspections for safety compliance

### Inspection Process
1. **Notice period** — 7 days advance notice via email and platform
2. **Scheduling** — You can request specific time slots
3. **Inspector access** — Provide reasonable access to property
4. **Report generation** — Digital inspection report within 48 hours
5. **Follow-up actions** — Address any violations within specified timeframe

### Inspection Results
Results appear in your property dashboard:
- **Compliant** — No issues found
- **Minor violations** — 30 days to correct
- **Major violations** — 14 days to correct
- **Safety violations** — Immediate attention required

### Requesting Inspections
You can request additional inspections:
1. Go to **"Maintenance"** → **"Request Inspection"**
2. Select **inspection type**:
   - Pre-improvement approval
   - Post-work completion
   - Insurance documentation
   - Sale preparation
3. Choose **preferred dates**
4. Submit request

## Managing Visitors

For community security, SuvrenHOA tracks visitor access to amenities and events.

### Registering Visitors
1. Go to **"Community"** → **"Visitor Management"**
2. Click **"Add New Visitor"**
3. Enter visitor details:
   - Full name
   - Relationship to you
   - Duration of visit
   - Amenities they'll access
4. Generate **visitor access code**
5. Share code with your visitor

### Visitor Privileges
Visitors can access:
- **Common areas** — Pool, playground, walking trails
- **Community events** — With homeowner supervision
- **Guest parking** — Limited duration
- **Clubhouse** — When accompanied by homeowner

Visitors cannot:
- Vote on proposals
- Submit maintenance requests
- Access financial information
- Use amenities without homeowner present

### Short-term vs Long-term Visitors
**Short-term** (under 14 days):
- Simple registration process
- Immediate access
- No additional fees

**Long-term** (over 14 days):
- Extended visitor fee applies
- Board notification required
- Temporary resident status may be needed

## Pet Registration

All pets must be registered with the HOA for insurance and community safety.

### Registering Your Pet
1. Go to **"My Property"** → **"Pet Registration"**
2. Upload **current vaccination records**
3. Provide **pet insurance information**
4. Add **emergency contact** (if you're not available)
5. Upload **recent photo** of pet
6. Pay **annual pet registration fee** in USDC

### Pet Information Required
- Pet name, breed, age, and weight
- Spay/neuter status
- Behavioral notes (friendly, shy, reactive)
- Special needs or medications
- Microchip information

### Community Pet Rules
- **Leash requirement** — All dogs on leash in common areas
- **Waste cleanup** — Owners responsible for cleanup
- **Noise restrictions** — No excessive barking/disturbance
- **Amenity access** — Pets allowed in designated areas only
- **Guest pets** — Must be registered as visitors

### Pet Violations
Common violations and penalties:
- **Unleashed pets** — $25 fine
- **Waste cleanup failure** — $50 fine
- **Noise complaints** — $25 first offense, $50 thereafter
- **Unregistered pets** — $100 fine plus registration requirement

## Vehicle Registration

All regularly parked vehicles must be registered for parking enforcement and emergency access.

### Registering Vehicles
1. Go to **"My Property"** → **"Vehicle Registration"**
2. Enter **license plate number**
3. Upload **registration document**
4. Select **primary parking location**
5. Designate as **resident** or **visitor** vehicle

### Vehicle Information
- Make, model, year, and color
- Primary driver name
- Insurance information
- Special accommodations needed

### Parking Rules
- **Assigned spots** — Each property has designated spaces
- **Visitor parking** — Limited to 72 consecutive hours
- **Street parking** — Follow local municipality rules
- **RV/boat storage** — Special areas and fees apply
- **Commercial vehicles** — Restricted in residential areas

### Parking Violations
- **Blocking access** — $100 fine, immediate tow risk
- **Unregistered vehicle** — $25 fine per day
- **Visitor overstay** — $50 fine
- **Assigned spot violation** — $25 fine

## Rental Management

If you rent out your property, special requirements apply.

### Registering as Rental Property
1. Go to **"My Property"** → **"Rental Status"**
2. Upload **rental agreement**
3. Provide **tenant contact information**
4. Submit **security deposit** to HOA (refundable)
5. Acknowledge **landlord responsibilities**

### Landlord Responsibilities
- **Tenant education** — Ensure tenants know HOA rules
- **Rule enforcement** — You're responsible for tenant violations
- **Communication** — Provide tenant contact to HOA
- **Maintenance** — Keep property in compliance
- **Access coordination** — Facilitate inspections and maintenance

### Tenant Rights and Restrictions
**Tenants can**:
- Use community amenities
- Attend community meetings (non-voting)
- Submit maintenance requests
- Participate in community events

**Tenants cannot**:
- Vote on proposals
- Run for board positions
- Access financial information
- Make property modifications

### Rental Violations
Landlords are responsible for all tenant violations. Repeated violations may result in:
- Increased security deposits
- Mandatory property management requirement
- Rental privilege suspension
- Legal action for continued non-compliance
