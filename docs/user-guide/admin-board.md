## Board Member Responsibilities

Detailed guide for elected board members on their duties, powers, and obligations.

### Officer Roles and Duties

**President**:
- **Executive leadership** — Overall direction and community representation
- **Meeting chair** — Leads board and annual meetings
- **External relations** — Primary contact with vendors, legal counsel, government
- **Emergency authority** — Can authorize emergency spending up to $5,000
- **Guardian powers** — Can reject proposals that violate bylaws
- **Legal representation** — Signs contracts and legal documents for HOA

**Vice President**:
- **President backup** — Acts as president when needed
- **Committee coordination** — Oversees special committees and projects
- **Community liaison** — Facilitates resident input and feedback
- **Proposal review** — Secondary review of community proposals
- **Event coordination** — Helps organize community events and meetings

**Treasurer**:
- **Financial oversight** — Manages budget, spending, and financial planning
- **Treasury management** — Oversees bank accounts and investments
- **Payment approval** — Authorizes routine expenditures and vendor payments
- **Financial reporting** — Prepares monthly and annual financial reports
- **Audit coordination** — Works with CPAs and auditors
- **Tax compliance** — Ensures proper tax filing and compliance

**Secretary**:
- **Record keeping** — Maintains all official HOA documents and records
- **Meeting minutes** — Records and publishes board meeting minutes
- **Correspondence** — Handles official HOA communications
- **Document management** — Organizes and maintains document library
- **Notice distribution** — Ensures proper notice of meetings and votes
- **Archive maintenance** — Preserves historical records

**Member-at-Large**:
- **General board duties** — Full voting member on all board decisions
- **Special projects** — Leads specific initiatives and improvements
- **Community representation** — Brings broader community perspective
- **Committee participation** — Serves on various committees as needed
- **Resident advocacy** — Advocates for resident interests and concerns

### Fiduciary Duties

**Duty of care**:
- **Informed decisions** — Make decisions based on adequate information
- **Business judgment** — Exercise reasonable business judgment
- **Professional advice** — Seek expert advice when needed
- **Meeting attendance** — Attend meetings and participate actively
- **Document review** — Read and understand all board materials

**Duty of loyalty**:
- **Community first** — Put community interests ahead of personal interests
- **Avoid conflicts** — Disclose and manage any conflicts of interest
- **Confidentiality** — Protect confidential information and executive session discussions
- **No personal benefit** — Don't use position for personal gain
- **Fair dealing** — Treat all residents fairly and equally

**Duty of obedience**:
- **Follow governing documents** — Comply with bylaws, CC&Rs, and policies
- **Legal compliance** — Ensure all actions comply with applicable laws
- **Mission adherence** — Stay focused on HOA's purpose and mission
- **Proper procedures** — Follow established procedures for all actions
- **Authority limits** — Stay within granted authority and don't exceed powers

### Meeting Management

**Board meeting responsibilities**:
- **Prepare agendas** — Plan meaningful and efficient meetings
- **Review materials** — Read all documents before meetings
- **Participate actively** — Contribute to discussions and decisions
- **Respect process** — Follow meeting procedures and parliamentary rules
- **Document decisions** — Ensure all decisions are properly recorded

**Executive session guidelines**:
- **Limited purposes** — Personnel, legal, contracts, enforcement only
- **Confidentiality** — Information discussed cannot be shared outside session
- **Documentation** — Keep general record of topics discussed
- **Return to open** — Reconvene in open session for any votes
- **Public reporting** — General summary of executive session topics

### Communication Responsibilities

**Community communication**:
- **Transparency** — Keep community informed of board activities
- **Accessibility** — Be available to residents for questions and concerns
- **Professional tone** — Maintain professional and respectful communication
- **Consistent messaging** — Coordinate with other board members on major announcements
- **Multiple channels** — Use platform, email, meetings, and forums for communication

**External communication**:
- **Designated spokesperson** — Usually president handles media and external requests
- **Legal consultation** — Involve attorney in legal matters and disputes
- **Vendor management** — Professional relationships with service providers
- **Government relations** — Maintain positive relationships with local officials

## Minting Property NFTs

Process for creating and issuing property NFTs for new properties or ownership changes.

### When to Mint New NFTs

**New construction**:
- **Subdivision development** — New lots added to community
- **Home construction** — New homes built on existing lots
- **Legal lot splits** — Property divided into multiple parcels
- **Annexation** — New properties added to HOA boundaries

**Property transfers requiring new NFTs**:
- **Lost access** — Original owner cannot access existing NFT
- **Estate situations** — Heirs need new NFT for inherited property
- **Legal judgments** — Court-ordered property transfers
- **Foreclosure recovery** — Bank-owned properties returning to private ownership

**System maintenance**:
- **Smart contract upgrades** — Migration to new NFT contract
- **Metadata updates** — Significant changes to property information
- **Security improvements** — Enhanced security features for NFTs

### Pre-Minting Requirements

**Property verification**:
1. **Legal description** — Survey and legal property description
2. **Title verification** — Clear title search and verification
3. **Zoning compliance** — Property meets zoning and HOA requirements
4. **Assessment completion** — Property assessed and added to tax rolls
5. **Infrastructure connection** — Utilities and services connected

**Documentation required**:
- **Deed and title documents** — Proof of ownership and legal status
- **Survey and plat map** — Official property boundaries and measurements
- **Property tax records** — Assessment and tax payment history
- **Utility connections** — Water, sewer, electric, gas connections verified
- **Building permits** — All construction properly permitted and inspected

### Minting Process

**Step 1: Property information gathering**
1. **Collect property details**:
   - Legal address and parcel number
   - Lot size and dimensions
   - Home square footage and features
   - Year built and major renovations
   - HOA assessments and fee structure

**Step 2: Metadata preparation**
1. **Create property metadata** including:
   - Property description and characteristics
   - Legal boundaries and restrictions
   - HOA obligations and fees
   - Historical information
   - Photos and documentation

**Step 3: Owner information verification**
1. **Verify new owner identity**:
   - Government-issued photo ID
   - Social Security number verification
   - Background check if required
   - Financial qualification verification
   - HOA application approval

**Step 4: NFT creation**
1. **Access NFT minting interface** — Board member portal
2. **Input property information** — All verified property details
3. **Upload metadata** — Property characteristics and documentation
4. **Set owner address** — New owner's verified wallet address
5. **Review and approve** — Final check before minting
6. **Execute minting** — Create NFT on blockchain
7. **Verify creation** — Confirm NFT created correctly

### Minting Authorization

**Authorization requirements**:
- **Board resolution** — Formal board vote authorizing minting
- **Signature requirements** — President and treasurer signatures required
- **Legal review** — Attorney approval for complex situations
- **Insurance verification** — Property adequately insured before minting

**Multi-signature process**:
1. **Primary authorization** — President initiates minting process
2. **Secondary approval** — Treasurer verifies financial and legal compliance
3. **Technical execution** — Secretary or designated tech admin executes minting
4. **Verification** — All board members confirm successful minting
5. **Documentation** — Complete record in board minutes and HOA records

### Post-Minting Procedures

**Owner notification**:
- **NFT delivery confirmation** — Verify owner received NFT in their wallet
- **Platform access** — Confirm new owner can access SuvrenHOA platform
- **Orientation scheduling** — Set up new owner orientation session
- **Welcome package** — Provide community information and resources

**Record keeping**:
- **NFT registry update** — Add new NFT to official HOA registry
- **Property database** — Update all property records and systems
- **Insurance notification** — Inform insurance company of new property
- **Tax notification** — Update property tax records if needed
- **Voting eligibility** — Confirm new owner can vote on proposals

### Error Prevention and Resolution

**Common minting errors**:
- **Wrong owner address** — NFT sent to incorrect wallet
- **Incorrect metadata** — Property information errors
- **Duplicate minting** — Accidentally creating multiple NFTs for same property
- **Invalid recipient** — Wallet address that can't receive NFTs

**Error resolution process**:
1. **Immediate assessment** — Determine scope and impact of error
2. **Stop further action** — Prevent additional errors or transactions
3. **Document error** — Record what happened and why
4. **Correction plan** — Develop plan to fix error
5. **Board approval** — Get approval for correction actions
6. **Execute correction** — Implement fix with proper authorization
7. **Verification** — Confirm error is fully corrected
8. **Process improvement** — Update procedures to prevent similar errors

## Managing User Roles

Administrative functions for managing resident accounts, permissions, and access levels.

### User Role Types

**Resident roles**:
- **Property Owner** — Full access to all platform features
- **Tenant** — Limited access, cannot vote or access financial information
- **Family Member** — Access to household account, limited permissions
- **Emergency Contact** — Access only during emergencies or owner authorization

**Administrative roles**:
- **Board Member** — Access to board functions and community oversight
- **President** — Additional executive powers and authorities
- **Treasurer** — Financial oversight and approval powers
- **Secretary** — Document management and communication powers
- **Committee Chair** — Limited administrative access for specific committees

**Service roles**:
- **Property Manager** — Professional management company access
- **Maintenance Staff** — Service request and work order management
- **Security Personnel** — Safety and security system access
- **Legal Counsel** — Access to legal documents and sensitive information

### User Account Creation

**New resident onboarding**:
1. **Account application** — Resident submits application with required documents
2. **Identity verification** — Photo ID, background check, reference verification
3. **Property verification** — Confirm legal ownership or lease agreement
4. **HOA compliance** — Verify dues current, no outstanding violations
5. **Account creation** — Set up platform account with appropriate permissions

**Required documentation**:
- **Government photo ID** — Driver's license or passport
- **Property documentation** — Deed, purchase agreement, or lease
- **Contact information** — Address, phone, email
- **Emergency contacts** — Family member or friend for emergencies
- **Background check** — Criminal history check if required by bylaws

### Permission Management

**Access level configuration**:

**Property Owner permissions**:
- View and edit personal property information
- Submit and track maintenance requests
- Participate in community forums and events
- Vote on proposals and elections
- Access financial reports and budget information
- Make dues payments and view payment history

**Tenant permissions**:
- View basic property information (no editing)
- Submit maintenance requests for urgent issues
- Participate in community forums and events
- Access community calendar and amenities
- No voting rights or financial information access

**Board member additional permissions**:
- Access all resident accounts and information
- Review and approve maintenance requests
- Manage vendor relationships and contracts
- Access detailed financial information and reports
- Approve or deny user account applications
- Manage community events and announcements

**Administrative functions**:
- Create and modify user accounts
- Assign and modify user permissions
- Reset passwords and recover accounts
- Generate user activity reports
- Manage platform settings and configurations

### Account Verification Process

**Initial verification**:
1. **Document review** — Check all submitted documentation for completeness
2. **Identity confirmation** — Verify ID documents are legitimate and current
3. **Property ownership** — Confirm legal right to property access
4. **Background check** — Criminal history and HOA compliance check
5. **Reference verification** — Contact previous landlords or HOA references

**Ongoing verification**:
- **Annual updates** — Require annual confirmation of contact information
- **Ownership changes** — Verify when property ownership transfers
- **Compliance monitoring** — Regular check of dues payment and violation status
- **Activity monitoring** — Watch for unusual account activity or violations

### Account Suspension and Termination

**Reasons for account suspension**:
- **Dues delinquency** — Over 90 days past due on payments
- **Violation of community rules** — Serious or repeated policy violations
- **Platform abuse** — Harassment, spam, or misuse of platform features
- **Security concerns** — Suspected account compromise or unauthorized access

**Suspension process**:
1. **Investigation** — Review account activity and gather evidence
2. **Warning notice** — Provide opportunity to correct issues
3. **Due process** — Allow resident to respond and appeal
4. **Board decision** — Formal vote on suspension action
5. **Suspension implementation** — Restrict account access with clear restoration criteria

**Account termination**:
- **Property sale/transfer** — Account closed when ownership transfers
- **Lease termination** — Tenant accounts closed when lease ends
- **Board decision** — Serious violations may result in permanent termination
- **Legal requirements** — Court orders or legal judgments requiring termination

### Data Privacy and Security

**Personal information protection**:
- **Minimum necessary** — Collect only information needed for HOA operations
- **Secure storage** — Encrypt sensitive data and use secure servers
- **Access controls** — Limit access to authorized personnel only
- **Regular audits** — Review access logs and security measures regularly

**Privacy policies**:
- **Transparent collection** — Clear notice of what information is collected
- **Purpose limitation** — Use information only for stated HOA purposes
- **Retention limits** — Delete information when no longer needed
- **Resident rights** — Allow residents to review and correct their information

## Emergency Spending Authority

Guidelines and procedures for emergency expenditures when immediate action is required.

### Emergency Spending Criteria

**Qualifying emergencies**:
- **Life safety issues** — Immediate danger to resident health or safety
- **Property protection** — Prevent significant property damage
- **Legal compliance** — Meet legal deadlines or court orders
- **Essential services** — Restore critical community services
- **Security breaches** — Address immediate security threats

**Examples of emergency spending**:
- **Pipe burst repair** — Major water leak causing property damage
- **Electrical hazards** — Dangerous electrical conditions requiring immediate repair
- **Security system failure** — Broken gates, cameras, or access control
- **Storm damage cleanup** — Remove fallen trees or repair storm damage
- **Legal emergency** — Immediate legal representation for HOA defense

### Authorization Levels

**President authority** — Up to $5,000:
- Can authorize immediate spending for true emergencies
- Must attempt to contact other board members when possible
- Required to report to full board within 24 hours
- Must provide detailed justification for spending

**Board majority** — $5,000 to $15,000:
- Requires approval from 3 of 5 board members
- Can be done via emergency phone meeting
- Must be documented with meeting minutes
- Full board ratification required at next meeting

**Full board** — $15,000 to $25,000:
- Requires emergency board meeting
- All 5 board members must participate
- Formal vote and detailed minutes required
- Community notification within 48 hours

**Community approval** — Over $25,000:
- Requires emergency community meeting
- 7-day notice to all residents
- Normal quorum and voting requirements apply
- Exception only for life-threatening emergencies

### Emergency Procedures

**Immediate response** (0-2 hours):
1. **Assess situation** — Determine if true emergency requiring immediate spending
2. **Life safety first** — Take immediate action to protect people
3. **Document everything** — Photos, contractor quotes, emergency justification
4. **Contact board** — Notify other board members as soon as possible
5. **Authorize spending** — Use appropriate authority level for amount needed

**Short-term follow-up** (2-24 hours):
1. **Report to board** — Full explanation of emergency and spending
2. **Gather documentation** — Invoices, receipts, contractor reports
3. **Plan communication** — Prepare community notification
4. **Assess additional needs** — Determine if more spending will be required
5. **Insurance notification** — Contact insurance company if applicable

**Formal documentation** (24-48 hours):
1. **Board meeting** — Emergency meeting to ratify spending
2. **Community notification** — Inform residents of emergency and spending
3. **Financial recording** — Proper accounting of emergency expenditures
4. **Insurance claims** — File claims for covered expenses
5. **Policy review** — Assess if policies need updating based on emergency

### Documentation Requirements

**Emergency authorization documentation**:
- **Justification memo** — Written explanation of emergency and spending need
- **Contractor quotes** — Multiple quotes when time permits
- **Photos and evidence** — Visual documentation of emergency condition
- **Timeline** — Sequence of events and decisions
- **Communication log** — Record of all board member consultations

**Financial documentation**:
- **Invoices and receipts** — Complete payment documentation
- **Budget impact** — How emergency affects annual budget
- **Funding source** — Which account emergency funds came from
- **Insurance coordination** — Claims filed and reimbursement expected
- **Cost-benefit analysis** — Why emergency spending was most cost-effective option

### Emergency Vendors

**Pre-approved emergency contractors**:
- **24/7 availability** — Contractors available for emergency calls
- **Insurance verification** — Current liability and workers compensation
- **Rate agreements** — Pre-negotiated emergency service rates
- **Quality assurance** — Proven track record of quality emergency work

**Emergency vendor list**:
- **Plumbing** — 24-hour plumbing service for water emergencies
- **Electrical** — Licensed electrician for electrical emergencies
- **HVAC** — Heating and cooling emergency repair service
- **Tree service** — Storm damage and dangerous tree removal
- **Security** — Emergency locksmith and security system repair
- **General contractor** — Emergency building repair and temporary protection

### Budget Impact and Recovery

**Budget adjustments**:
- **Reserve fund use** — Draw from reserves for major emergencies
- **Budget reallocation** — Move money from non-essential categories
- **Special assessment** — Community vote for emergency assessment if needed
- **Insurance recovery** — Coordinate insurance reimbursement

**Cost recovery strategies**:
- **Insurance claims** — Pursue all available insurance coverage
- **Vendor warranties** — Use vendor guarantees for equipment failures
- **Legal recovery** — Pursue recovery from liable parties when applicable
- **Preventive measures** — Invest in prevention to avoid future emergencies

## Proposal Guardian Powers

Authority and procedures for board intervention in community proposals.

### Guardian Power Authority

**Legal basis**:
- **Fiduciary duty** — Board obligation to protect community interests
- **Bylaw compliance** — Ensure all proposals comply with governing documents
- **Legal protection** — Prevent proposals that would create legal liability
- **Financial oversight** — Protect community from financially harmful proposals

**Types of guardian powers**:
- **Mandatory rejection** — Must reject proposals that violate laws or bylaws
- **Discretionary rejection** — May reject proposals that harm community interests
- **Modification authority** — Can require changes before allowing vote
- **Delay authority** — Can postpone votes to allow for legal review

### Mandatory Rejection Criteria

**Legal violations**:
- **Federal law violations** — Proposals violating federal regulations
- **State law violations** — Conflicts with state homeowner association laws
- **Local ordinances** — Violations of city or county regulations
- **Fair housing violations** — Discriminatory policies or practices

**Governing document violations**:
- **Bylaw conflicts** — Proposals contradicting existing bylaws
- **CC&R violations** — Changes requiring different approval process
- **Policy conflicts** — Contradictions with existing board policies
- **Charter violations** — Conflicts with HOA articles of incorporation

**Financial authority violations**:
- **Budget excess** — Spending beyond board's financial authority
- **Debt creation** — Creating debt without proper approval
- **Contract violations** — Conflicting with existing vendor contracts
- **Fiduciary breaches** — Proposals harming community financial interests

### Discretionary Rejection Criteria

**Community safety concerns**:
- **Physical safety risks** — Proposals creating danger to residents
- **Security vulnerabilities** — Changes that compromise community security
- **Emergency access** — Proposals blocking emergency vehicle access
- **Insurance impacts** — Changes that could void community insurance

**Property value protection**:
- **Aesthetic compatibility** — Proposals harming community appearance
- **Market value impact** — Changes that could reduce property values
- **Maintenance burdens** — Proposals creating excessive maintenance costs
- **Quality standards** — Changes below community quality expectations

### Guardian Process Procedures

**Proposal review timeline**:
1. **Initial submission** — Resident submits proposal through platform
2. **Staff review** — Administrative review for completeness (2 business days)
3. **Board review** — Board assessment of guardian power issues (5 business days)
4. **Legal consultation** — Attorney review if needed (3 business days)
5. **Decision notification** — Resident notified of approval or rejection (1 business day)

**Rejection process**:
1. **Draft rejection notice** — Board prepares detailed explanation
2. **Legal review** — Attorney reviews rejection reasoning
3. **Board vote** — Formal board vote on rejection
4. **Written notification** — Detailed rejection notice to resident
5. **Appeal opportunity** — Information on appeal process provided

### Appeal Process

**Appeal timeline**:
- **Appeal deadline** — 30 days from rejection notification
- **Board review** — 60 days for board to reconsider decision
- **Community option** — Resident may request community override vote
- **Legal challenge** — Court review available for legal disputes

**Appeal procedures**:
1. **Written appeal** — Resident submits formal appeal with reasoning
2. **New information** — Any new facts or legal arguments
3. **Board reconsideration** — Full board reviews original decision
4. **Public hearing** — Opportunity for resident to present case
5. **Final decision** — Board vote on appeal with detailed reasoning

**Community override**:
- **Petition requirement** — 25% of properties must petition for override vote
- **Special meeting** — Community meeting called to consider override
- **Supermajority required** — 75% vote required to override board decision
- **Legal protection** — Override cannot violate laws or mandatory rejections

### Guardian Power Limitations

**Cannot reject**:
- **Constitutional amendments** — Community has final say on fundamental changes
- **Board criticism** — Proposals critical of board performance
- **Political expression** — First amendment protected speech and assembly
- **Personal preferences** — Board's personal opinions on non-governance matters

**Transparency requirements**:
- **Public reasoning** — All rejections must include detailed public explanation
- **Legal basis** — Clear citation of legal or bylaw basis for rejection
- **Alternative suggestions** — When possible, suggest modifications that would be acceptable
- **Annual reporting** — Report to community on all guardian power uses

### Best Practices for Guardian Powers

**Before exercising guardian powers**:
- **Seek legal advice** — Consult attorney for complex legal issues
- **Document reasoning** — Prepare detailed written justification
- **Consider alternatives** — Look for ways to modify rather than reject
- **Community impact** — Assess how rejection affects community trust

**Communication standards**:
- **Respectful tone** — Professional and respectful communication
- **Educational approach** — Explain legal and practical concerns
- **Solution-oriented** — Suggest modifications when possible
- **Transparent process** — Clear explanation of decision-making process

## Elections Management

Board responsibilities for managing fair and transparent community elections.

### Election Planning and Preparation

**Annual election timeline**:
- **January** — Begin election planning, form election committee
- **February** — Open nomination period, distribute candidate information
- **March** — Close nominations, verify candidate eligibility
- **April** — Conduct voting, hold candidate forums
- **May** — Announce results, begin board transition

**Election committee formation**:
- **Committee composition** — 3 residents plus 1 board member
- **Chair selection** — Volunteer resident serves as committee chair
- **Responsibilities** — Oversee fair and transparent election process
- **Authority** — Make decisions on election procedures and disputes
- **Accountability** — Report to board and community on election activities

### Candidate Management

**Eligibility verification**:
1. **Property ownership** — Verify current property ownership
2. **Good standing** — Confirm dues current, no major violations
3. **Residency requirement** — Confirm candidate meets residency requirements
4. **Background check** — Review for any disqualifying criminal history
5. **Conflict assessment** — Identify any potential conflicts of interest

**Candidate support services**:
- **Information sessions** — Explain board duties and election process
- **Platform submission** — Help candidates prepare platform statements
- **Forum scheduling** — Organize candidate forums and debates
- **Technical assistance** — Help with platform technology and submissions
- **Equal treatment** — Ensure all candidates receive same opportunities

### Voting System Management

**Blockchain voting setup**:
1. **Smart contract deployment** — Deploy voting smart contract
2. **Candidate registration** — Add all verified candidates to voting system
3. **Voter eligibility** — Verify all property owners eligible to vote
4. **Security testing** — Test voting system for security and accuracy
5. **Backup procedures** — Plan for technical failures or disputes

**Voting period management**:
- **Opening announcement** — Notify all residents when voting opens
- **Daily monitoring** — Track participation and system performance
- **Technical support** — Assist residents with voting difficulties
- **Participation tracking** — Monitor turnout and encourage participation
- **Security oversight** — Watch for any voting irregularities or attacks

### Vote Counting and Results

**Automated counting process**:
1. **Vote collection** — Smart contract automatically collects all votes
2. **Ranked choice calculation** — System calculates results using ranked choice rules
3. **Result verification** — Election committee verifies automated results
4. **Audit trail** — Review blockchain records for accuracy
5. **Result publication** — Announce results to community

**Transparency measures**:
- **Public blockchain** — All votes recorded on public blockchain
- **Verification tools** — Residents can verify their votes were counted
- **Detailed results** — Round-by-round results published
- **Statistical analysis** — Participation and demographic analysis
- **Post-election report** — Comprehensive report on entire election process

### Election Security

**Voter verification**:
- **Eligibility confirmation** — Verify voter owns property and is in good standing
- **One vote per property** — Prevent multiple voting from same property
- **Identity protection** — Votes are anonymous but verifiable
- **Coercion prevention** — Measures to prevent vote buying or coercion

**System security**:
- **Smart contract audit** — Professional security review of voting code
- **Network monitoring** — Watch for attacks on voting system
- **Backup systems** — Alternative voting methods if primary system fails
- **Incident response** — Plan for responding to security incidents

### Election Disputes

**Common dispute types**:
- **Candidate eligibility** — Challenges to candidate qualifications
- **Voting irregularities** — Claims of voting system problems
- **Campaign violations** — Allegations of improper campaigning
- **Result challenges** — Disputes over vote counting or results

**Dispute resolution process**:
1. **Formal complaint** — Written complaint filed with election committee
2. **Investigation** — Committee investigates allegations
3. **Evidence review** — All relevant evidence collected and reviewed
4. **Committee decision** — Committee makes ruling on dispute
5. **Appeal process** — Option to appeal to full board or independent arbitrator

**Remedies available**:
