- **Suspicious activity** — Unknown persons, unusual behavior
- **Trespassing** — Unauthorized individuals in community

**Disturbances**:
- **Noise violations** — Loud parties, construction outside hours
- **Domestic disputes** — Arguments visible to neighbors
- **Pet problems** — Aggressive animals, barking, unleashed pets
- **Traffic violations** — Speeding, reckless driving, parking violations

### Incident Reporting Process

**For emergencies requiring immediate response**:
1. **Call 911 first** — Life, property, or safety in immediate danger
2. **Notify security service** — Call monitoring service hotline
3. **Alert board member** — Contact on-call board member
4. **Document incident** — File report after emergency is handled

**For non-emergency incidents**:
1. Go to **"Safety"** → **"Report Incident"**
2. **Select incident type** — Choose from categorized list
3. **Provide detailed information**:
   - Date, time, and exact location
   - Detailed description of what occurred
   - People involved or witnessed incident
   - Photos or videos if available
   - Immediate actions taken
4. **Submit report** — System generates incident number for tracking

### Information Required

**Basic incident details**:
- **Date and time** — When incident occurred
- **Location** — Specific address or area description
- **Incident type** — Category and subcategory
- **Severity level** — Low, medium, high, emergency
- **People involved** — Names and contact info (if known)

**Detailed description**:
- **What happened** — Chronological sequence of events
- **Witnesses** — Anyone who saw the incident
- **Property damage** — Description of any damage
- **Injuries** — Medical attention needed or received
- **Photos/video** — Visual documentation of incident/damage

**Follow-up information**:
- **Police report** — Report number if police were called
- **Insurance claims** — If property damage occurred
- **Medical treatment** — If anyone required medical care
- **Ongoing concerns** — Whether issue might happen again

### Incident Response

**Immediate response** (emergency incidents):
- **Emergency services** — 911 contacted by reporting party
- **Security notification** — Monitoring service dispatched
- **Board notification** — On-call board member alerted
- **Community notification** — Alert sent if community-wide concern

**Standard response** (non-emergency incidents):
- **Acknowledgment** — Incident report confirmed within 2 hours
- **Investigation** — Board member or security investigates within 24 hours
- **Communication** — Update provided within 48 hours
- **Resolution** — Action taken and incident closed

**Follow-up procedures**:
- **Incident analysis** — Review of what happened and why
- **Prevention measures** — Steps to prevent similar incidents
- **Policy updates** — Rules or procedures changed if needed
- **Community education** — Safety tips or awareness campaigns

### Incident Tracking

## Noise Complaints

Process for addressing noise issues while maintaining community harmony.

### Noise Regulations

**Quiet hours**:
- **Weekdays** — 10 PM to 7 AM
- **Weekends** — 11 PM to 8 AM
- **Holidays** — 11 PM to 9 AM
- **Pool area** — 9 PM closing during all days

**Acceptable noise levels**:
- **Daytime** — Normal household activities, conversation
- **Evening** — Reduced volume for TV, music, voices
- **Night** — Only necessary household sounds
- **Early morning** — Minimal noise until 7 AM weekdays, 8 AM weekends

### Types of Noise Issues

**Common noise complaints**:
- **Loud music** — Stereos, parties, musical instruments
- **Television** — Volume too high, especially late night
- **Voices** — Loud talking, yelling, outdoor conversations
- **Dogs barking** — Persistent or excessive barking
- **Vehicle noise** — Loud engines, car stereos, motorcycles
- **Construction** — Outside permitted hours or too loud

**Permitted noise activities**:
- **Construction** — 8 AM - 6 PM weekdays, 9 AM - 5 PM weekends
- **Lawn maintenance** — 8 AM - 8 PM weekdays, 9 AM - 7 PM weekends
- **Pool activities** — During open hours with reasonable volume
- **Children playing** — Reasonable play noise during daytime hours

### Complaint Process

**Step 1: Direct communication** (preferred):
1. **Approach neighbor directly** — Polite conversation about noise
2. **Explain the impact** — How noise affects you
3. **Suggest solutions** — Working together to reduce noise
4. **Set expectations** — Agree on acceptable noise levels
5. **Follow up** — Check if agreed solutions are working

**Step 2: Formal complaint**:
1. Go to **"Safety"** → **"Noise Complaint"**
2. **Document the noise**:
   - Date, time, and duration
   - Type of noise and volume level
   - Impact on your household
   - Previous attempts to resolve
   - Witnesses to the noise
3. **Submit complaint** with any audio/video evidence
4. **Receive confirmation** — Complaint logged and tracked

**Step 3: HOA investigation**:
- **Initial contact** — Board member contacts noise source within 24 hours
- **Neighbor notification** — Alleged violator informed of complaint
- **Investigation** — Board member may observe or gather more information
- **Mediation offer** — Opportunity for neighbors to work out solution
- **Decision** — Determination of violation and any penalties

### Resolution Approaches

**Mediation services**:
- **Neutral mediator** — Trained volunteer to facilitate discussion
- **Structured conversation** — Organized approach to problem-solving
- **Win-win solutions** — Finding arrangements that work for everyone
- **Agreement documentation** — Written agreement if solution reached

**Enforcement actions**:
- **First violation** — Written warning and education about noise rules
- **Second violation** — $50 fine and required community service
- **Third violation** — $100 fine and mandatory mediation
- **Ongoing violations** — Escalating fines and potential legal action

### Special Circumstances

**Temporary noise situations**:
- **Moving activities** — One-time loud activities with advance notice
- **Special events** — Parties or gatherings with prior approval
- **Emergency situations** — Necessary noise for safety reasons
- **Construction projects** — Major improvements with proper permits

**Noise variance requests**:
1. **Submit request** 7 days before planned activity
2. **Describe activity** — What will be happening and expected noise level
3. **Propose mitigation** — Steps to minimize noise impact
4. **Neighbor notification** — Adjacent properties informed of variance
5. **Board approval** — Decision made within 3 business days

### Prevention and Education

**Noise awareness**:
- **Community guidelines** — Clear explanation of noise expectations
- **New resident orientation** — Noise rules explained to all new residents
- **Regular reminders** — Periodic communication about noise courtesy
- **Positive reinforcement** — Recognition for quiet, considerate neighbors

**Sound management tips**:
- **Indoor noise** — Rugs, curtains, and furniture to absorb sound
- **Outdoor considerations** — Awareness of sound travel
- **Technology solutions** — Sound machines, earplugs for sensitive residents
- **Timing awareness** — Scheduling noisy activities during appropriate hours

## Emergency Procedures

Detailed procedures for various emergency situations that may affect the community.

### Fire Emergency

**If you discover a fire**:
1. **Call 911 immediately** — Report fire location and size
2. **Alert neighbors** — Knock on doors and shout warnings
3. **Evacuate safely** — Leave building immediately, don't use elevators
4. **Meet at rally point** — Gather at designated safe location
5. **Stay out** — Don't re-enter until fire department gives all-clear

**Fire evacuation routes**:
- **Primary routes** — Main roads and sidewalks out of community
- **Secondary routes** — Alternate paths if primary routes blocked
- **Rally points** — Clubhouse parking lot and pavilion area
- **Assembly areas** — Designated areas for residents to gather safely

### Severe Weather

**Tornado warning procedures**:
1. **Seek immediate shelter** — Go to lowest floor, interior room
2. **Avoid windows** — Stay away from glass and exterior walls
3. **Cover yourself** — Use blankets, pillows, or mattresses for protection
4. **Monitor alerts** — Keep battery radio or phone for updates
5. **Stay sheltered** — Until all-clear is given by authorities

**Severe thunderstorm safety**:
- **Go indoors** — Seek substantial shelter immediately
- **Avoid water** — Stay away from plumbing and electrical appliances
- **Unplug electronics** — Protect from power surges
- **Wait for all-clear** — Stay inside until storm passes completely

**Flooding procedures**:
- **Monitor conditions** — Watch for rising water levels
- **Evacuate if necessary** — Leave area if flooding threatens homes
- **Avoid flood waters** — Never drive or walk through flooded areas
- **Emergency supplies** — Keep emergency kit accessible

### Medical Emergencies

**For life-threatening emergencies**:
1. **Call 911 immediately** — Request paramedics and ambulance
2. **Provide first aid** — If trained, provide appropriate care
3. **Guide emergency services** — Meet paramedics at community entrance
4. **Notify emergency contact** — Contact family member or friend
5. **Follow up** — Ensure person receives appropriate continuing care

**Community AED locations**:
- **Clubhouse** — Main entrance area
- **Pool facility** — Poolside equipment room
- **Maintenance building** — Emergency equipment storage

### Power Outages

**During extended power outages**:
- **Check on neighbors** — Especially elderly or disabled residents
- **Use flashlights** — Avoid candles due to fire risk
- **Conserve phone battery** — Limit non-essential phone use
- **Food safety** — Keep refrigerators closed to preserve food
- **Generator safety** — Use generators outdoors only, never inside

**Community response**:
- **Emergency lighting** — Battery backup lights activate automatically
- **Communication** — Battery-powered PA system for community updates
- **Shelter availability** — Clubhouse available as warming/cooling center
- **Welfare checks** — Security personnel check on vulnerable residents

### Security Threats

**If you observe suspicious activity**:
1. **Don't confront** — Observe from a safe distance
2. **Call security** — Contact monitoring service immediately
3. **Call 911** — If immediate danger to person or property
4. **Document details** — Note descriptions, license plates, directions
5. **Stay safe** — Protect yourself and family first

**Active threat situations**:
- **Run** — Evacuate if possible, using fastest route
- **Hide** — If escape not possible, find secure hiding place
- **Fight** — As last resort, defend yourself if directly threatened
- **Call 911** — When safe to do so, contact emergency services

### Chemical/Hazmat Emergencies

**Gas leaks**:
1. **Evacuate immediately** — Leave area without delay
2. **Avoid ignition sources** — No smoking, electrical switches, or phones
3. **Call 911** — From safe location away from leak
4. **Alert neighbors** — Warn others in potentially affected area
5. **Stay upwind** — Position yourself where wind carries gas away

**Chemical spills**:
- **Avoid contact** — Don't touch or inhale unknown substances
- **Evacuate area** — Move away from spill location
- **Call emergency services** — 911 for immediate response
- **Provide information** — Any known details about the substance

### Communication During Emergencies

**Emergency notification system**:
- **Mass alerts** — Text, email, and app notifications to all residents
- **Severity coding** — Different alert types for different emergencies
- **Update frequency** — Regular updates as situations develop
- **All-clear notification** — Official notice when emergency is over
