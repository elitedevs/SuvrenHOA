## Maintenance Requests

Report issues and request services through the integrated maintenance system.

### Types of Maintenance Requests

**Common Area Maintenance**:
- Landscaping and grounds issues
- Pool and recreational facility problems
- Lighting outages or electrical issues
- Plumbing problems in common areas
- Damage to sidewalks, roads, or parking areas
- Playground and amenity equipment issues

**Emergency Requests**:
- Safety hazards requiring immediate attention
- Water leaks affecting multiple properties
- Electrical hazards in common areas
- Storm damage cleanup
- Security system failures
- Any issue posing immediate danger to residents

**Routine Services**:
- Pressure washing requests
- Tree trimming and removal
- Gutter cleaning and maintenance
- Exterior building maintenance
- Common area cleaning
- Equipment servicing and inspections

### Submitting Maintenance Requests

**Step 1: Create Request**
1. Go to **"Maintenance"** → **"Submit Request"**
2. **Select request type**:
   - Emergency (immediate attention)
   - Urgent (within 24 hours)
   - Standard (within 5-7 business days)
   - Routine (within 2 weeks)
3. **Choose category** from dropdown menu
4. **Describe the issue** in detail

**Step 2: Provide Details**
- **Location information** — Exact location of problem
- **Photos** — Upload up to 5 clear photos of issue
- **Safety concerns** — Any immediate hazards
- **Preferred contact method** — Phone, email, or platform messaging
- **Availability** — When you're available if access needed

**Step 3: Submit and Track**
- **Review request** before submitting
- **Receive confirmation** with ticket number
- **Track progress** in real-time
- **Receive updates** via chosen communication method

### Priority Levels

**Emergency** (Response within 2 hours):
- Safety hazards, water leaks, electrical dangers
- Security breaches or system failures
- Situations requiring immediate board attention
- After-hours contact: Emergency hotline available

**Urgent** (Response within 24 hours):
- Non-emergency safety issues
- Amenity failures affecting community events
- Significant property damage
- Issues affecting multiple properties

**Standard** (Response within 5-7 business days):
- Routine repairs and maintenance
- Non-critical system issues
- Landscaping and cosmetic improvements
- Equipment maintenance and servicing

**Routine** (Response within 2 weeks):
- Preventive maintenance tasks
- Non-urgent cosmetic improvements
- Long-term planning items
- Vendor contract renewals

### Request Tracking

**Real-time status updates**:
- **Submitted** — Request received and logged
- **In Review** — Board or management reviewing request
- **Approved** — Work authorized and scheduled
- **In Progress** — Work underway
- **Completed** — Work finished, pending resident confirmation
- **Closed** — Request resolved and confirmed

**Communication updates**:
- **Email notifications** — Automatic updates on status changes
- **SMS alerts** — For urgent and emergency requests
- **Platform notifications** — Updates visible when you log in
- **Detailed logs** — Full history of all communications and actions

### Resident Responsibilities

**When submitting requests**:
- **Provide accurate information** — Detailed, truthful descriptions
- **Include photos** — Clear images help contractors understand issue
- **Be available** — Respond to follow-up questions promptly
- **Verify completion** — Confirm work was completed satisfactorily

**During work**:
- **Provide reasonable access** — Allow contractors into common areas
- **Report safety concerns** — Alert management to any safety issues
- **Document issues** — Take photos if work quality is unsatisfactory
- **Communicate changes** — Notify management if needs change

## Parking Management

Comprehensive parking system for residents, guests, and service providers.

### Parking Assignments

**Resident parking**:
- **Assigned spaces** — Each property gets 2 designated parking spaces
- **Garage access** — Properties with garages must use them
- **Additional spaces** — Available for purchase ($25/month)
- **Parking permits** — Vehicle registration required

**Guest parking**:
- **Visitor spaces** — Clearly marked areas throughout community
- **Time limits** — 72 consecutive hours maximum
- **Permit requirements** — Guest permits required for overnight parking
- **Holiday restrictions** — Limited guest parking during major holidays

**Service provider parking**:
- **Contractor spaces** — Designated areas for service vehicles
- **Delivery zones** — Short-term parking for packages
- **Emergency access** — Always kept clear for emergency vehicles
- **Commercial restrictions** — Overnight commercial parking prohibited

### Parking Permits

**Resident vehicle registration**:
1. Go to **"Property"** → **"Vehicle Registration"**
2. **Enter vehicle details** — License plate, make, model, color
3. **Upload registration** — Current vehicle registration document
4. **Select parking space** — Choose from available assignments
5. **Pay annual fee** — $25 per vehicle

**Guest permits**:
- **Temporary permits** — Available through platform or clubhouse
- **Duration options** — 1 day, 3 days, or 1 week
- **Cost**: Free for first 5 permits per month, $5 each thereafter
- **Display required** — Must be visible in guest vehicle

**Service provider permits**:
- **Contractor registration** — Vendors must register vehicles
- **Work order required** — Permit linked to specific maintenance request
- **Time restrictions** — Valid only during scheduled work hours
- **Cleanup requirement** — Responsible for any spills or debris

### Parking Enforcement

**Violation types and fines**:
- **No permit displayed** — $25 fine
- **Parking in fire lane** — $100 fine, immediate tow risk
- **Blocking access** — $75 fine, immediate tow risk
- **Overnight guest parking** — $50 fine per night
- **Commercial vehicle violation** — $100 fine
- **Abandoned vehicle** — $200 fine plus tow costs

**Enforcement process**:
1. **Warning issued** — First violation gets written warning
2. **Fine assessment** — Second violation results in fine
3. **Payment required** — 30 days to pay through platform
4. **Escalation** — Unpaid fines added to property dues
5. **Towing authorized** — For safety violations or repeat offenses

**Appeal process**:
- **Submit appeal** within 10 days of citation
- **Provide evidence** — Photos, receipts, or other documentation
- **Board review** — Appeals considered at next board meeting
- **Decision final** — Board decision on appeals is binding

### Special Parking Situations

**RV and boat storage**:
- **Designated area** — Special storage section of community
- **Monthly fee** — $75/month for RV, $50/month for boat
- **Size restrictions** — Maximum 35 feet length
- **Registration required** — Annual registration and insurance proof

**Motorcycle and bicycle storage**:
- **Motorcycle spaces** — Smaller designated areas
- **Bicycle racks** — Available at various locations
- **Security measures** — Covered areas with lighting
- **No additional fee** — Included in regular parking allocation

**Disability accommodations**:
- **ADA spaces** — Wider spaces near building entrances
- **Reserved parking** — Available with medical documentation
- **No additional cost** — Accommodations provided at no charge
- **Enforcement priority** — Violations strictly enforced

## Architectural Review

All exterior modifications require approval to maintain community standards and property values.

### What Requires Approval

**Requires approval**:
- Exterior paint color changes
- Roof replacements or major repairs
- Deck, patio, or porch additions
- Fence installation or modifications
- Landscaping changes (trees, major plantings)
- Exterior lighting additions
- Satellite dish or antenna installation
- Mailbox or signage changes
- Driveway or walkway modifications

**Does not require approval**:
- Interior modifications (not visible from street)
- Routine maintenance and repairs
- Seasonal decorations (temporary)
- Plantings in existing landscape areas
- Minor exterior repairs using same materials
- Storm door or screen door installation

### Application Process

**Step 1: Prepare Application**
1. Go to **"Maintenance"** → **"Architectural Review"**
2. **Complete application** with detailed project description
3. **Upload required documents**:
   - Detailed plans or drawings
   - Material specifications and colors
   - Contractor information and permits
   - Photos of current condition
4. **Pay application fee** — $50 for most projects

**Step 2: Review Process**
- **Initial review** — Staff checks application completeness
- **Neighbor notification** — Adjacent properties informed of application
- **Committee review** — Architectural review committee evaluates project
- **Site visit** — Committee may visit property for complex projects
- **Decision rendered** — Approval, conditional approval, or denial

**Step 3: Implementation**
- **Approval valid** for 6 months from date issued
- **Work begins** only after permit approval
- **Inspection required** — Committee inspects completed work
- **Final approval** — Project officially approved upon completion

### Review Criteria

**Aesthetic compatibility**:
- **Harmony with neighborhood** — Fits existing architectural style
- **Color coordination** — Compatible with approved community palette
- **Material quality** — Meets community standards for durability
- **Design appropriateness** — Suitable for property type and location

**Community standards**:
- **Property value impact** — Enhancement or neutral effect on values
- **Neighbor consideration** — Minimal negative impact on adjacent properties
- **Maintenance requirements** — Reasonable long-term maintenance needs
- **Safety considerations** — Meets all safety and building codes

### Common Approval Guidelines

**Paint colors**:
- **Earth tones preferred** — Beiges, browns, grays, and muted colors
- **Bright colors discouraged** — Neon, fluorescent, or highly saturated colors
- **Trim coordination** — Trim colors must complement main color
- **Historical accuracy** — Respect original architectural style

**Landscaping**:
- **Native plants encouraged** — Drought-resistant and low-maintenance
- **Mature tree protection** — Cannot remove large trees without approval
- **Water-wise design** — Efficient irrigation and appropriate plantings
- **Seasonal interest** — Year-round appeal preferred

**Structures and additions**:
- **Setback compliance** — Must meet all zoning requirements
- **Height restrictions** — Cannot exceed established limits
- **Material matching** — New work should complement existing structure
- **Professional installation** — Licensed contractors required for major work

### Appeal Process

**If application is denied**:
1. **Request detailed explanation** — Written reasons for denial
2. **Modify application** — Address specific concerns raised
3. **Resubmit application** — No additional fee for modifications
4. **Formal appeal** — Request board review of committee decision
5. **Board hearing** — Present case to full board

**Appeal timeline**:
- **Initial decision** — 30 days from complete application
- **Appeal filing** — Must appeal within 30 days of denial
- **Board review** — Within 60 days of appeal filing
- **Final decision** — Board decision is binding

## Irrigation Systems

### System Components

**Zone configuration**:
- **12 irrigation zones** — Different areas with specific watering needs
- **Smart controllers** — Weather-responsive automatic scheduling
- **Drip irrigation** — Efficient watering for flower beds and trees
- **Spray irrigation** — Coverage for lawn areas
- **Rain sensors** — Automatic shutoff during precipitation

**Water sources**:
- **Municipal water** — Primary source for all irrigation
- **Well water** — Backup source for emergency use
- **Rainwater collection** — Supplemental source from clubhouse roof
- **Recycled water** — Considering purple pipe for future expansion

### Irrigation Scheduling

**Seasonal adjustments**:
- **Spring** (March-May): 3 times per week, 15 minutes per zone
- **Summer** (June-August): Daily watering, 20 minutes per zone
- **Fall** (September-November): 2 times per week, 10 minutes per zone
- **Winter** (December-February): Weekly watering, 5 minutes per zone

**Zone-specific scheduling**:
- **Lawn areas** — Deep, infrequent watering for root development
- **Flower beds** — Daily light watering during growing season
- **Trees and shrubs** — Deep watering 2-3 times per week
- **Slopes and difficult areas** — Multiple short cycles to prevent runoff

### Water Conservation

**Efficiency measures**:
- **Smart irrigation controllers** — Automatically adjust for weather
- **Pressure regulation** — Optimal pressure for efficient coverage
- **Regular maintenance** — Fix leaks and broken sprinkler heads promptly
- **Landscaping design** — Native and drought-resistant plants

**Conservation goals**:
- **25% reduction** in water usage from baseline year
- **Drought compliance** — Follow municipal water restrictions
- **Rainwater harvesting** — Capture and use natural precipitation
- **Education programs** — Teach residents about water conservation

### System Maintenance

**Regular maintenance tasks**:
- **Monthly inspections** — Check all zones for proper operation
- **Seasonal adjustments** — Update schedules for weather changes
- **Repair work** — Fix broken heads, pipes, and controllers

**Professional services**:
