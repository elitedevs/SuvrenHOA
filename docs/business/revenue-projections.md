## Assumptions & Methodology

These projections are built bottom-up from realistic customer acquisition rates for a solo founder with a bootstrap budget. No venture capital. No paid sales team. No $10k/mo ad spend.

The US has ~350,000 HOAs managing ~40 million homes. North Carolina alone has ~15,000+ HOAs. The Triangle area (Raleigh/Durham/Chapel Hill) has several hundred active HOAs. This is not a market problem — it's a sales and trust problem.

### Core Assumptions

**Infrastructure Costs (Monthly)**
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Supabase (Pro tier) | $25/mo |
| Vercel Pro | $20/mo |
| Domain(s) | ~$3/mo amortized |
| Resend (email) | $0–20/mo (free tier covers early growth) |
| Gas relayer funding (Base L2) | $5–15/mo at scale |
| AI/OpenAI API (assistant feature) | $10–50/mo depending on usage |
| Misc (monitoring, CDN, etc.) | $10–20/mo |
| **Total infra burn** | **~$75–150/mo** |

**Blended ARPU (Average Revenue Per Unit — i.e., per community)**

Based on expected tier mix:
- Starter (self-managed small HOAs): ~40% of customers → $39/mo avg (annual)
- Professional (mid-size or more engaged): ~45% → $99/mo avg (annual)
- Enterprise (large/mgmt companies): ~15% → $199/mo avg (annual)

**Weighted ARPU = (0.40 × $39) + (0.45 × $99) + (0.15 × $199) = $15.60 + $44.55 + $29.85 = ~$90/mo per customer**

## Churn Analysis

HOA management software generally has **low natural churn** because:
1. Switching costs are high (community needs to re-onboard, re-migrate data)
2. HOA boards change slowly (elections every 1–3 years)
3. Once homeowners are on-chain (wallets, NFTs), migration is emotionally harder

**Expected churn rates by stage:**

| Stage | Monthly Churn | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Month-to-month | 5–8% | Worst case — still figuring out the product |
| After 6 months | 3–5% | Some stickiness developing |
| Annual contracts | 1–2% | Board changes being primary driver |
| Enterprise / mgmt co | <1% | Contract + integration creates strong lock-in |

**Annual churn (roughly):**
- Early monthly customers: ~45–65% annual churn (brutal, but manageable if CAC is low)
- Mixed monthly/annual: ~25–35% annual churn
- Mostly annual contracts: ~12–20% annual churn (healthy SaaS territory)

**Strategy:** Push hard for annual subscriptions at signup. The 20–25% discount is the incentive. Annual contracts also signal serious customers and reduce billing overhead.

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

For a solo founder with bootstrap budget:

| Channel | Est. CAC | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| In-person / word of mouth (Raleigh) | $0–50 | Time cost only; this is how Year 1 happens |
| Content marketing (SEO blog) | $50–200 | 6–12 month lag; then very low ongoing CAC |
| HOA conference booth | $500–1,500 per event | 10–25 leads per event; CAC ~$150–500 |
| Property manager partnerships | $0–200 | Partnership deal = bulk customers with near-zero per-unit CAC |
| Direct outreach (email/LinkedIn) | $25–100 | Time-intensive but scalable |
| Paid ads (if profitable) | $200–500 | Not recommended Year 1 — too little data to optimize |

**Target CAC: Under $200 for Year 1–2.** At $90/mo ARPU and 24-month retention, LTV is ~$2,160 — a 10:1 LTV:CAC is very healthy.

## What Could Kill This

Be honest with yourself about the risks:

1. **Slow blockchain adoption:** HOA boards skew older. Wallet setup is a real friction point. Mitigate with gasless UX and Coinbase Wallet integration (most user-friendly).
2. **Regulatory surprise:** If a state AG decides blockchain voting doesn't meet HOA statute requirements, you have a legal problem. Mitigate with HOA attorney review and conservative "advisory vote" framing initially.
3. **Competition:** Entrenched players (AppFolio, Buildium) could build blockchain features. They probably won't quickly — their codebases are monolithic and their customers are management companies, not HOA boards. But it's a risk after Year 2–3 if you get traction.
4. **Solo founder burnout:** This is the #1 risk. Keep scope tight, use automation, don't chase every feature request.
