Here's what's in there and the key decisions that came out of the research:
Critical finding: use timestamp-based clock mode on Base. block.number on L2s is unreliable — it can reference L1 block numbers or have inconsistent timing. Both PropertyNFT and the Governor override clock() to return block.timestamp, and voting delays/periods are specified in seconds. This is non-negotiable on an Optimism-stack L2.
Auto-delegation on mint. OZ's ERC721Votes requires explicit delegate() before voting power activates. If we don't handle this, every homeowner would have to call delegate(self) before they can vote — instant UX death for non-crypto users. The spec has _delegate(to, to) in mintProperty() at ~$0.0005 extra gas. Worth every wei.
Per-category quorum is custom. We're NOT using GovernorVotesQuorumFraction because it's a single percentage. Instead, we override _quorumReached() and _voteSucceeded() to check the proposal's category — Routine at 15%, Constitutional at 67% with 2/3 supermajority.
web3.storage is dead. Free tier is gone, migrated to Storacha. For IPFS pinning at your volume (~20 docs/year), Tarlo at €5/month or Filebase at $20/month are the picks.
