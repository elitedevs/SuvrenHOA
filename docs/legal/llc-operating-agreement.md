# OPERATING AGREEMENT OF SUVREN LLC

**A North Carolina Limited Liability Company**

---

## RECITALS

This Operating Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into and effective as of the date of formation, by and between **Suvren LLC**, a North Carolina limited liability company (the "Company"), and **Ryan Shanahan** (the "Member"), who is the sole member of the Company.

---

## ARTICLE I — FORMATION

**1.1 Formation.** The Company has been organized as a North Carolina Limited Liability Company under and pursuant to the North Carolina Limited Liability Company Act (N.C.G.S. Chapter 57D) (the "Act") by the filing of Articles of Organization with the North Carolina Secretary of State.

**1.2 Name.** The name of the Company is **Suvren LLC**.

**1.3 Principal Office.** The principal place of business of the Company shall be located at such place as the Member may designate from time to time.

**1.4 Registered Agent.** The Company shall maintain a registered agent in North Carolina as required by the Act. The registered agent may be changed by the Member at any time upon proper filing with the North Carolina Secretary of State.

**1.5 Term.** The Company shall commence upon the filing of the Articles of Organization and shall continue in perpetuity unless dissolved in accordance with the provisions of this Agreement or the Act.

---

## ARTICLE II — PURPOSE

**2.1 General Purpose.** The purpose of the Company is to engage in any lawful business activity permitted under the laws of North Carolina, including but not limited to:

- Software development and engineering services
- Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) product development and licensing
- Blockchain governance solutions and platforms
- HOA management technology products (including SuvrenHOA)
- Consulting, advisory, and professional services related to the foregoing
- All activities reasonably related or incidental to the foregoing

**2.2 Specific Products.** The Company's primary product focus includes the SuvrenHOA platform — a blockchain-native homeowner association governance solution — and any derivative or related products.

---

## ARTICLE III — MEMBERSHIP

**3.1 Sole Member.** The Company has one (1) member: **Ryan Shanahan** (the "Member"), who holds one hundred percent (100%) of the membership interest in the Company.

**3.2 Membership Interest.** The Member's ownership interest represents 100% of all economic rights, voting rights, and governance rights of the Company.

**3.3 No Transfer Without Amendment.** The Member may not transfer, sell, assign, pledge, or otherwise dispose of any membership interest without amending this Agreement to reflect such transfer.

**3.4 No Additional Members.** No additional members shall be admitted to the Company without the written consent of the Member and a formal amendment to this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE IV — CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

**4.1 Initial Contribution.** The Member's initial capital contribution to the Company consists of cash, services, intellectual property, and/or other assets as agreed upon and recorded in the Company's books and records.

**4.2 Additional Contributions.** The Member may, but is not required to, make additional capital contributions to the Company at any time. No additional contributions shall be required without the Member's express written consent.

**4.3 Capital Account.** The Company shall maintain a capital account for the Member, to which all contributions shall be credited and all distributions and losses shall be debited.

**4.4 Return of Capital.** The Member shall not be entitled to receive a return of any capital contribution except upon dissolution of the Company or as otherwise determined by the Member.

---

## ARTICLE V — ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**5.1 Allocations.** As the sole member, all profits, losses, credits, and other items of Company income shall be allocated 100% to the Member.

**5.2 Distributions.** The Member may cause the Company to make distributions of cash or other assets at such times and in such amounts as the Member shall determine in their sole discretion, subject to:

  (a) The Company maintaining adequate reserves for current and anticipated obligations;  
  (b) The restrictions of the Act regarding distributions; and  
  (c) Any applicable loan covenants or third-party agreements.

**5.3 No Guaranteed Return.** The Member is not entitled to any guaranteed return on their capital contribution or any guaranteed distribution.

---

## ARTICLE VI — MANAGEMENT

**6.1 Member-Managed.** The Company shall be **member-managed**. The Member shall have full and complete authority, power, and discretion to manage and control the business affairs and operations of the Company.

**6.2 Authority of Member.** Without limiting the foregoing, the Member shall have the authority to:

  (a) Execute contracts, agreements, and instruments on behalf of the Company;  
  (b) Open and manage bank accounts and financial accounts;  
  (c) Hire and terminate employees, independent contractors, and agents;  
  (d) Acquire, hold, lease, mortgage, sell, or otherwise deal with Company property;  
  (e) Borrow money and issue debt instruments on behalf of the Company;  
  (f) Initiate or defend legal proceedings on behalf of the Company;  
  (g) Make tax elections on behalf of the Company;  
  (h) Admit or remove members (by amendment to this Agreement);  
  (i) Take any other action necessary or appropriate for the operation of the Company.

**6.3 Authority to Bind.** The Member is authorized to bind the Company in any contract or agreement, and to execute documents on behalf of the Company as its authorized representative.

**6.4 Compensation.** The Member may receive reasonable compensation from the Company for services rendered, as determined by the Member from time to time.

**6.5 Expenses.** The Company shall reimburse the Member for all reasonable and necessary expenses incurred in connection with Company business.

---

## ARTICLE VII — BOOKS, RECORDS, AND ACCOUNTING

**7.1 Books and Records.** The Company shall maintain accurate and complete books and records of all financial transactions and business activities, including:

  (a) A current list of the full name and last known address of the Member;  
  (b) A copy of the Articles of Organization and all amendments;  
  (c) A copy of this Operating Agreement and all amendments;  
  (d) Copies of income tax returns and financial statements for the three most recent years;  
  (e) A writing setting out the amount of cash and description of other property contributed by the Member.

**7.2 Fiscal Year.** The Company's fiscal year shall be the calendar year (January 1 – December 31), unless the Member elects otherwise.

**7.3 Accounting Method.** The Company shall use the cash method or accrual method of accounting as determined by the Member and as required by applicable tax law.

**7.4 Bank Accounts.** The Company shall maintain one or more bank accounts in the Company's name. Company funds shall not be commingled with the personal funds of the Member.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — TAXES

**8.1 Tax Classification.** As a single-member LLC, the Company shall be treated as a disregarded entity for federal income tax purposes unless the Member makes a valid election to be taxed as a corporation. The Member shall report Company income and losses on Schedule C (or Schedule E, as applicable) of the Member's individual federal income tax return (Form 1040).

**8.2 Tax Elections.** The Member shall have the authority to make all tax elections on behalf of the Company, including elections under the Internal Revenue Code.

**8.3 Self-Employment Tax.** The Member acknowledges that net earnings from the Company's business activities are subject to self-employment tax under applicable law.

---

## ARTICLE IX — LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

**9.1 Limitation of Liability.** The Member shall not be personally liable for any debt, obligation, or liability of the Company solely by reason of being a member, except as otherwise required by the Act.

**9.2 Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Member from and against any and all claims, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or related to any act or omission made by the Member on behalf of the Company, provided that:

  (a) The Member acted in good faith and in a manner the Member reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Company;  
  (b) The claim, liability, or expense did not arise from the Member's gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud; and  
  (c) The indemnification is not prohibited by applicable law.

**9.3 Insurance.** The Company may, in the discretion of the Member, purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of the Member against any liability arising out of or related to the Member's role in the Company.

---

## ARTICLE X — DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

**10.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following:

  (a) The written determination of the Member to dissolve the Company;  
  (b) The occurrence of any event that requires dissolution under the Act; or  
  (c) Entry of a judicial decree of dissolution under the Act.

**10.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Member (or a person appointed by the Member or a court) shall wind up the Company's affairs by:

  (a) Completing unfinished business;  
  (b) Collecting amounts owed to the Company;  
  (c) Paying or providing for all Company liabilities and obligations; and  
  (d) Distributing remaining assets to the Member.

**10.3 Priority of Distribution.** Upon dissolution, Company assets shall be distributed in the following order:

  1. Payment of creditors (other than the Member);  
  2. Payment of any Company obligations to the Member acting as creditor;  
  3. Remaining assets to the Member.

**10.4 Articles of Dissolution.** Upon completion of winding up, the Member shall file Articles of Dissolution with the North Carolina Secretary of State as required by the Act.

---

## ARTICLE XI — AMENDMENTS

**11.1 Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by the Member. Any amendment shall be attached to and made a part of this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE XII — MISCELLANEOUS

**12.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of North Carolina, without regard to its conflict of laws provisions.

**12.2 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement of the Member with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and representations.

**12.3 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**12.4 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.

**12.5 Electronic Signatures.** Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding to the same extent as original signatures.

**12.6 Headings.** Section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.
