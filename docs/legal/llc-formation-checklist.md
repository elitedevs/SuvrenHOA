# North Carolina LLC Formation Checklist
## Suvren LLC — Step-by-Step Guide

## ✅ STEP-BY-STEP FORMATION CHECKLIST

---

### STEP 1 — Choose Your LLC Name
- [ ] Confirm name is available: [NC Secretary of State Business Search](https://www.sosnc.gov/online_services/search/by_title/_Business_Registration)
- [ ] Name must include "LLC", "L.L.C.", "Limited Liability Company", or similar
- [ ] Optional: Reserve the name for 120 days (Form BE-13, $30 fee) if not filing immediately
- [ ] Check federal/state trademark conflicts at [USPTO.gov](https://www.uspto.gov)

**Your LLC name:** Suvren LLC ✓

---

### STEP 2 — Appoint a Registered Agent
- [ ] Every NC LLC must have a registered agent with a physical NC address (no PO boxes)
- [ ] Agent must be available during normal business hours to receive legal documents
- [ ] Options:
  - **You** (if you have a NC physical address you're comfortable making public)
  - **Northwest Registered Agent** — ~$125/yr ⭐ *Recommended for privacy*
  - **CT Corporation, Registered Agents Inc., LegalZoom** — $100–300/yr
- [ ] Note: Using a registered agent service keeps your home address off public records

**Recommendation:** Northwest Registered Agent (~$125/yr) — privacy-first, no upsells, quality compliance calendar

---

### STEP 3 — File Articles of Organization
- [ ] File online at [NC Secretary of State](https://www.sosnc.gov) (fastest — usually same day)
- [ ] Or mail Form L-01 to: Secretary of State, PO Box 29622, Raleigh, NC 27626
- [ ] **Filing fee: $125** (one-time, non-refundable)
- [ ] Required information:
  - LLC name
  - Registered agent name and NC address
  - Principal office address
  - Organizer name and signature
- [ ] Save the stamped/filed copy — you'll need it to open a bank account

**Processing time:** Online filing typically approved same business day

---

### STEP 4 — Draft Your Operating Agreement
- [ ] NC does not require filing an operating agreement, but you must have one
- [ ] Keep it internal — signed and stored with your business records
- [ ] Defines: ownership, management, distributions, dissolution, and member rights
- [ ] Even as a single-member LLC, this document:
  - Protects your liability shield
  - Proves the LLC is legitimate to banks and investors
  - Governs operations if ever challenged

**Status:** Template operating agreement created ✓ (have attorney review before signing)

---

### STEP 5 — Get Your EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- [ ] Apply free at [IRS.gov/EIN](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online)
- [ ] Takes ~15 minutes online — EIN issued immediately
- [ ] Required for:
  - Opening a business bank account
  - Hiring employees or contractors (1099s)
  - Filing business taxes
  - Many vendor/payment processor applications
- [ ] Even as a single-member LLC with no employees, get an EIN (do not use your SSN for business)
- [ ] **Cost: Free**

---

### STEP 6 — Open a Business Bank Account
- [ ] **Required documents:**
  - Articles of Organization (stamped copy from NC SOS)
  - EIN confirmation letter from IRS
  - Operating Agreement
  - Government-issued ID
- [ ] Recommended banks for small tech businesses:
  - **Mercury Bank** (online, no fees, great for SaaS/startups) ⭐
  - **Relay Financial** (online, excellent expense tracking)
  - **First Citizens Bank** (NC-based, good local relationships)
  - Chase/Wells Fargo (traditional, fee-based but widely accepted)
- [ ] **Never commingling personal and business funds** — this is the #1 way to pierce the LLC veil
- [ ] Set up a separate business credit card for expenses

---

### STEP 7 — Register for NC State Taxes (if applicable)
- [ ] If selling taxable goods/services in NC: Register for Sales & Use Tax at [NC DOR](https://www.ncdor.gov)
- [ ] SaaS products: NC taxes SaaS under "digital property" — consult a CPA
- [ ] If you have NC employees: Register for withholding tax
- [ ] Single-member LLC default: Pass-through taxation (no separate entity-level income tax)

---

### STEP 8 — Obtain Business Licenses and Permits
- [ ] **Federal:** No general federal business license required for software/SaaS
- [ ] **State of NC:**
  - No general state business license required
  - Check profession-specific requirements at [NC Business Link](https://www.ncbusiness.nc.gov)
- [ ] **Wake County / Raleigh (if operating locally):**
  - City of Raleigh Business License: Not required (Raleigh eliminated general business licenses)
  - Wake County: No general county business license for most businesses
  - Check zoning if operating from home: [Raleigh Unified Development Ordinance](https://www.raleighnc.gov/permits-licenses)
- [ ] **Home-based business:** Generally allowed in Raleigh for non-client-facing software businesses

**For SuvrenHOA specifically:** No special licenses required for software/SaaS in NC. If acting as a financial custodian of HOA funds, consult an attorney about fiduciary requirements.

---

### STEP 9 — File Annual Report
- [ ] **Due date:** April 15 each year
- [ ] **Fee:** $200/year
- [ ] File online at [NC Secretary of State](https://www.sosnc.gov)
- [ ] First annual report due April 15 of the year following formation
- [ ] Failure to file results in administrative dissolution
- [ ] Set a calendar reminder now: **April 15, annually**

---

### STEP 10 — Set Up Accounting & Tax Systems
- [ ] Choose accounting software:
  - **Wave** (free, good for early stage)
  - **QuickBooks Self-Employed** (~$15/mo, integrates with TurboTax)
  - **FreshBooks** (~$17/mo, better for invoicing)
- [ ] Track all business income and expenses from day one
- [ ] Tax treatment for single-member LLC:
  - Treated as **disregarded entity** by default
  - All income/losses reported on **Schedule C** of your personal Form 1040
  - Subject to **self-employment tax** (15.3% on net earnings up to threshold)
  - Consider making **quarterly estimated tax payments** (Form 1040-ES) to avoid penalties
- [ ] Consider S-Corp election if income exceeds ~$50K/year (potential SE tax savings — consult CPA)

## ⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Commingling funds** — Always keep personal and business money separate
2. **Missing the annual report** — $200/yr, administrative dissolution if missed
3. **Using SSN instead of EIN** — Get an EIN even if you have no employees
4. **No operating agreement** — Banks and courts will question your LLC's legitimacy without one
5. **Home address on public record** — Use a registered agent service for privacy
6. **Ignoring quarterly taxes** — Self-employment income is not withheld; pay estimated taxes quarterly
